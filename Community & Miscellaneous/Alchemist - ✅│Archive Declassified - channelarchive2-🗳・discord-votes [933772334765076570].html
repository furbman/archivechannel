@Zoocrane Eyes On Crucible Challenge: Where are we currently: The Crucible as an NFT is innovative itself and arguably has more depths with its features (financially and visually) than many other NFTs. It was originally created as a contruct that allows you to subscribe to different reward programs. What is the problem (particularly for this challenge): People outside of the current alchemyst ecosystem do not know about the crucible and its features and do not care about the ‘artistic’ (referring to the visual and collectible properties) of the crucible NFT Why does it matter? The NFT crowd is very strong in spreading hype and engagement on platforms like twitter once it gains interest, we need them to start caring about the crucible. If people do not care about it they will not engage/buy/spread the word about it and we will never gain traction in a broader audience (here the NFT ‘collector’ audience) What would need to happen? One thing that came to my mind when I see what makes the NFT crowd go crazy is comparable rarity and trait combinations of their collectibles or signals like the PFP. For this the crucible does not check the box for me yet, they are hard to compare visually at first glance and then I am not sure what is rare and desirable. How could we achieve this? I would consider listing the crucible on a site like like https://rarity.tools/ where users are basically told what is rare, what is considered rare and ultimately what can be considered “grail” crucibles. This sort of narrative and transparency is needed for a larger audience to create and feel hype around collecting or engaging with a NFT. Is it a lot of work? I am not sure about this one and how much back and forth this would require with a site like rarity.tools and how much it would cost. I do believe that compared to implementing difficult technical changes to the crucible etc. this could be considered a “lower” effort but high impact strategic initiative.
