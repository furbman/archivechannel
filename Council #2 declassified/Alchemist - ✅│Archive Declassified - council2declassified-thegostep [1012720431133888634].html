thegostep 27-Aug-22 07:58 PM
I was asked by council members to provide my assessments of the areas where the council can enact change in order to improve the future of alchemist. Here are some thoughts. Council compensation and incentive alignment If alchemist wishes to attract and retain top talent to the council, it must find a way to compensate this position that aligns incentives with the community that elected them. This likely means receiving some compensation from inflation which is vested and or tied to key objectives the council is undertaking. One suggestion is that if a council member refers a project that goes on to return significant value to the DAO, this council member ought to gain from the success. Note that the compensation needs to be carefully thought through based on the role the council plans to have. Receiving outside help The council will not always be subject matter experts and should be focused on finding and retaining the best people it can to help figure things out. The council should have some budgets to be able to hire consultants and or contractors to do this work. Background checks on council members Given all the allegations that have been floating around and the mess that the whole Sifu situation caused, it is probably a good idea for council members to elect to do background checks. This can be done in a way that protects privacy of individuals by using some third party investigation firm and lawyer to custody the private information and only reveal if the background check came back with anything unexpected. This is in line with the objective of the council to be beyond criticism. Council charter and legal structure Alchemist would benefit from clearly defining its governance structure with a charter and constitution. This would define the responsibilities and duties of council members and the process by which they are elected or removed. This would also allow the dao to have a legal structure and engage in legal agreements with other parties. A constitution would define the principles that govern the dao and which the council or some other governance structure must interpret and enact. Alchemist lore The most important asset that alchemist has is its lore. Communicating the vision of alchemist and the story is what will attract other builders to join on the mission and propel it to the next level. Alchemist has not done a good job translating the vision into something which is accessible to the community. Council multisig Alchemist has various on-chain access control which should all be in the control of the council. This includes multisig and ownership over other contracts. I believe to this date I am still controlling some contracts on behalf of the council and it would be best for it all to be documented and controlled by a council multisig directly.
